## ARTICLE II - SHAREHOLDERS

### Section 2.1 - Annual Meeting
An annual meeting of shareholders shall be held each year at a date, time, and place determined by the Board of Directors, for the purpose of electing directors and transacting such other business as may properly come before the meeting.

**Default Date:** First Monday of December
**Default Time:** 10:00 AM Mountain Time
**Default Location:** Principal office or via remote communication

### Section 2.2 - Special Meetings
Special meetings of shareholders may be called by:
- The President
- The Board of Directors
- Shareholders holding not less than 10% of all outstanding shares entitled to vote

### Section 2.3 - Notice of Meetings
Written or electronic notice of all meetings of shareholders shall be given not less than 10 days nor more than 60 days before the date of the meeting. Notice shall include:
- Date, time, and place of meeting
- Purpose of meeting (for special meetings)
- Record date for determination of shareholders entitled to vote
- Means of remote communication (if applicable)

### Section 2.4 - Quorum
A majority of the outstanding shares entitled to vote, represented in person or by proxy, shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business at any meeting of shareholders.

### Section 2.5 - Voting Rights
Each outstanding share of Common Stock shall be entitled to one vote on each matter submitted to a vote at a meeting of shareholders.

**Voting Methods:**
- In person
- By proxy
- By remote communication (telephone, internet, video conference)

### Section 2.6 - Action Without Meeting
Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of shareholders may be taken without a meeting if written consent is signed by shareholders holding a majority of shares entitled to vote on the action.

### Section 2.7 - Remote Participation
Shareholders may participate in meetings by means of remote communication, including telephone, internet, or video conference, provided all participants can hear each other simultaneously.

---

## ARTICLE III - BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 3.1 - Powers and Duties
The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board of Directors, which shall have all powers permitted by Wyoming law.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Set corporate strategy and policy
- Approve annual budgets and major expenditures
- Appoint and supervise officers
- Authorize issuance of stock
- Declare dividends
- Approve contracts and agreements
- Oversee compliance and risk management

### Section 3.2 - Number and Term
The Board shall consist of not less than one (1) and not more than nine (9) directors as determined from time to time by resolution of the Board or shareholders.

**Initial Board:** One (1) director
**Term:** Directors shall serve until the next annual meeting of shareholders or until their successors are elected and qualified.

### Section 3.3 - Qualifications
Directors need not be shareholders of the Corporation or residents of Wyoming.

### Section 3.4 - Election and Removal
- **Election:** Directors shall be elected by a plurality of votes cast at the annual meeting of shareholders.
- **Removal:** Directors may be removed with or without cause by vote of shareholders holding a majority of shares entitled to vote.

### Section 3.5 - Vacancies
Vacancies on the Board may be filled by the remaining directors, even if less than a quorum, or by the shareholders. A director elected to fill a vacancy shall serve until the next annual meeting.

### Section 3.6 - Regular Meetings
Regular meetings of the Board may be held without notice at such times and places as determined by resolution of the Board.

**Default:** Quarterly on the first Monday of January, April, July, and October at 10:00 AM MT.

### Section 3.7 - Special Meetings
Special meetings of the Board may be called by the President or any two directors upon at least 48 hours' notice to each director.

### Section 3.8 - Notice
Notice of special meetings shall state the date, time, place, and purpose of the meeting. Notice may be given by:
- Personal delivery
- Mail
- Telephone
- Email or other electronic means

### Section 3.9 - Waiver of Notice
A director may waive notice of any meeting before, during, or after the meeting. Attendance at a meeting constitutes waiver of notice unless the director objects at the beginning of the meeting.

### Section 3.10 - Quorum and Voting
A majority of the directors then in office shall constitute a quorum. The act of a majority of directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board.

### Section 3.11 - Remote Participation
Directors may participate in meetings by means of conference telephone, video conference, or similar communications equipment by which all persons participating can hear each other simultaneously.

### Section 3.12 - Action Without Meeting
Any action required or permitted to be taken by the Board may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing or by electronic transmission to the action.

### Section 3.13 - Committees
The Board may establish committees and delegate authority to such committees as permitted by Wyoming law. Each committee shall have at least one director and shall report to the Board.

**Standing Committees (as needed):**
- Executive Committee
- Audit Committee
- Compensation Committee
- Compliance Committee

### Section 3.14 - Compensation
Directors may receive reasonable compensation for their services as determined by the Board. Directors may be reimbursed for expenses incurred in performing their duties.

## ARTICLE IV - OFFICERS

### Section 4.1 - Required Officers
The Corporation shall have the following officers:
- **President** (Chief Executive Officer)
- **Secretary**
- **Treasurer** (Chief Financial Officer)

### Section 4.2 - Additional Officers
The Board may appoint additional officers, including:
- Vice Presidents
- Assistant Secretaries
- Assistant Treasurers
- Chief Operating Officer
- General Counsel
- Other officers as deemed necessary

### Section 4.3 - Election and Term
Officers shall be elected by the Board at the annual meeting of the Board following the annual meeting of shareholders. Officers shall serve at the pleasure of the Board and until their successors are elected and qualified.

### Section 4.4 - Removal
Any officer may be removed by the Board with or without cause at any time.

### Section 4.5 - Vacancies
Vacancies in any office may be filled by the Board.

### Section 4.6 - President (Chief Executive Officer)
The President shall be the chief executive officer of the Corporation and shall:
- Supervise and control all business and affairs
- Preside at meetings of shareholders and Board
- Execute contracts, deeds, and instruments on behalf of the Corporation
- Perform all duties incident to the office and as assigned by the Board

### Section 4.7 - Secretary
The Secretary shall:
- Keep minutes of meetings of shareholders and Board
- Maintain corporate records and stock ledger
- Give notices as required by law and these Bylaws
- Authenticate corporate records
- Perform all duties incident to the office and as assigned

### Section 4.8 - Treasurer (Chief Financial Officer)
The Treasurer shall:
- Have custody of all corporate funds and securities
- Keep full and accurate accounts of receipts and disbursements
- Deposit funds in corporate depositories
- Render financial reports to Board and shareholders
- Perform all duties incident to the office and as assigned

### Section 4.9 - Delegation
Officers may delegate their duties to subordinates, subject to Board approval, but remain responsible for proper performance.

### Section 4.10 - Compensation
Officers shall receive such compensation as determined by the Board.

## ARTICLE V - STOCK

### Section 5.1 - Authorized Capital
The Corporation is authorized to issue **10,000,000 shares of Common Stock**, no par value.

### Section 5.2 - Issuance
Shares may be issued by the Board for such consideration as the Board deems adequate. The Board may issue shares in exchange for:
- Cash
- Property
- Services rendered
- Promissory notes
- Other consideration

### Section 5.3 - Stock Certificates
The Corporation may issue certificated or uncertificated shares. Certificates shall be signed by the President and Secretary and shall state:
- Corporation name
- Shareholder name
- Number and class of shares
- Par value (if any)

### Section 5.4 - Transfer of Shares
**General Rule:** Shares shall be freely transferable subject to these Bylaws and applicable securities laws.

**Restrictions on Transfer:**
The Board may impose reasonable restrictions on transfer of shares, including:
- Right of first refusal
- Approval of Board required
- Lock-up periods
- Restrictions to maintain S-Corporation status (if elected)

**Transfer Procedure:**
Shares may be transferred by:
1. Surrender of certificate (if certificated)
2. Proper endorsement or stock power
3. Compliance with any transfer restrictions
4. Notation on corporate books

### Section 5.5 - Lost Certificates
Upon proof of loss or destruction of a stock certificate and provision of indemnity satisfactory to the Corporation, a new certificate may be issued.

### Section 5.6 - Record Date
The Board may fix a record date not more than 70 days nor less than 10 days before:
- Any meeting of shareholders
- Payment of any dividend
- Any action requiring determination of shareholders

### Section 5.7 - Dividends
The Board may declare and pay dividends from time to time out of legally available funds, subject to restrictions in the Articles of Incorporation and applicable law.

## ARTICLE VI - INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 6.1 - Mandatory Indemnification
The Corporation shall indemnify any person who was or is a party to any proceeding (other than an action by or in the right of the Corporation) by reason of the fact that the person is or was a director, officer, employee, or agent of the Corporation, against expenses (including attorneys' fees), judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred, if the person acted in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Corporation.

### Section 6.2 - Permissive Indemnification
The Corporation may indemnify any person in any proceeding to the fullest extent permitted by Wyoming law.

### Section 6.3 - Advancement of Expenses
The Corporation shall advance expenses incurred in defending any proceeding to any person entitled to indemnification under this Article, upon receipt of an undertaking to repay such advances if it is ultimately determined that indemnification is not required.

### Section 6.4 - Insurance
The Corporation may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any person entitled to indemnification against any liability asserted against or incurred by such person.

### Section 6.5 - Non-Exclusivity
The indemnification and advancement of expenses provided by this Article shall not be exclusive of any other rights to which a person may be entitled under law, agreement, vote of shareholders or Board, or otherwise.

## ARTICLE VII - FISCAL YEAR

The fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year (January 1 - December 31), unless otherwise determined by the Board.

## ARTICLE VIII - CORPORATE SEAL

The Corporation may have a corporate seal, which shall be in such form as adopted by the Board. The seal may be used by causing it to be impressed or affixed or reproduced.

---

## ARTICLE IX - AMENDMENTS

### Section 9.1 - By Shareholders
These Bylaws may be altered, amended, or repealed and new bylaws may be adopted by vote of shareholders holding a majority of shares entitled to vote.

### Section 9.2 - By Board
The Board may amend these Bylaws unless the Articles of Incorporation or Wyoming law reserves that power exclusively to the shareholders. Any amendment by the Board may be altered or repealed by the shareholders.

### Section 9.3 - Notice
Notice of any meeting at which Bylaws are to be amended shall include the text of the proposed amendment or a summary of the changes.

---

## ARTICLE X - DISSOLUTION

### Section 10.1 - Voluntary Dissolution
The Corporation may be dissolved by:
- Written consent of all shareholders, or
- Vote of shareholders holding a majority of shares entitled to vote at a meeting called for that purpose

### Section 10.2 - Procedure
Upon dissolution:
1. Corporation shall cease carrying on business except as necessary for winding up
2. Board or appointed liquidators shall wind up affairs
3. Assets shall be distributed:
   - First, to pay or provide for payment of all debts and obligations
   - Second, to establish reserves for contingent liabilities
   - Third, remainder to shareholders pro rata according to their shareholdings

### Section 10.3 - Filing
Articles of Dissolution shall be filed with the Wyoming Secretary of State as required by law.

---

## ARTICLE XI - MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

### Section 11.1 - Books and Records
The Corporation shall keep at its principal office:
- Articles of Incorporation and Bylaws
- Minutes of shareholders and Board meetings
- List of shareholders and their addresses
- Financial records and statements
- Such other records as required by Wyoming law

### Section 11.2 - Inspection Rights
Shareholders shall have inspection rights as provided by Wyoming law.

### Section 11.3 - Execution of Documents
All deeds, mortgages, bonds, contracts, checks, drafts, and other documents of the Corporation shall be executed by such officers or agents as authorized by the Board.

### Section 11.4 - Banking
Corporate funds shall be deposited in such banks or other depositories as the Board may designate. Withdrawals shall be made by such persons as authorized by the Board.

### Section 11.5 - Loans to Officers and Directors
The Corporation may lend money to or guarantee obligations of officers and directors only upon approval by a disinterested majority of the Board.

### Section 11.6 - Related Party Transactions
Any transaction between the Corporation and a director, officer, or their affiliates must be:
- Disclosed to the Board
- Approved by disinterested directors
- Fair and reasonable to the Corporation

### Section 11.7 - Conflict of Interest
Directors and officers shall disclose any conflicts of interest. A director with a conflict may not vote on the matter but may be counted for quorum purposes.

### Section 11.8 - Electronic Communications
The Corporation may use electronic means for any notice, communication, or action under these Bylaws or Wyoming law, provided the method reasonably ensures authenticity and receipt.

### Section 11.9 - Gender and Number
Words in any gender shall include all genders. Singular includes plural and vice versa.

### Section 11.10 - Headings
Article and section headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

### Section 11.11 - Severability
If any provision of these Bylaws is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### Section 11.12 - Governing Law
These Bylaws shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wyoming.

---

## ARTICLE XII - EMERGENCY PROVISIONS

### Section 12.1 - Emergency Defined
An emergency exists if a quorum of directors cannot be readily assembled because of a catastrophic event, including but not limited to natural disaster, war, civil unrest, or pandemic.

### Section 12.2 - Emergency Powers
During an emergency:
- Any available director(s) may act for the full Board
- Notice requirements may be reduced or waived
- Meetings may be held by any available means of communication
- The principal office may be temporarily relocated
- Officers may take necessary actions to preserve corporate assets

### Section 12.3 - Termination
Emergency provisions terminate when a quorum of the Board can be assembled or when declared ended by the available directors.
